About MobyGlobal

Our Mission

MobyGlobal was created to help protect and study whale populations through non-invasive monitoring technology. Our network of acoustic sensors and AI-driven detection algorithms allow us to track whale movements in real-time, providing valuable data for conservation efforts and research.

The Technology

Our system uses a network of underwater hydrophones to capture ocean sounds. These audio signals are processed through a specialized neural network trained to identify whale vocalizations with over 95% accuracy. The data is then transmitted to our central server where it's displayed on our interactive map.

TensorFlow Python Flask Machine Learning IoT

Conservation Impact

The real-time data provided by MobyGlobal helps:

  • Alert shipping vessels to whale presence, reducing ship strikes
  • Monitor migration patterns and habitat usage
  • Track population changes over time
  • Identify areas that need enhanced protection

The Team

MobyGlobal is a ISEF 2025 project developed by Matthew Li and Taha Rawjani, a team of students passionate about marine conservation and technology. Our interdisciplinary team brings together expertise in software development, electronics, marine biology, and data science.
